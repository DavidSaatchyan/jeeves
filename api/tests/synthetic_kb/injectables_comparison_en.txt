Injectable Treatments Comparison: Profhilo vs Sculptra

Profhilo is a hyaluronic acid skin booster designed to improve skin hydration, elasticity, and overall quality. It is injected using a specific technique called BAP (Bio Aesthetic Points) with five injection points on each side of the face. Profhilo is particularly effective for treating skin laxity on the face, neck, décolletage, and hands. A standard treatment course consists of two sessions spaced one month apart, with results lasting approximately 6 months. Maintenance sessions every 6 months are recommended. Profhilo does not provide volume restoration — it is strictly a bio-remodelling treatment that stimulates collagen and elastin production through hydration. Price: €350–€450 per session.

Sculptra is a poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA) collagen stimulator used for volume restoration and deep wrinkle correction. It is indicated for patients with significant facial volume loss due to ageing, particularly in the mid-face, temples, and jawline. Sculptra works by stimulating the body's own collagen production over time. A typical treatment course requires 2 to 3 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart. Results appear gradually over 3–6 months and can last up to 2 years. Sculptra is also used off-label for buttock augmentation and body contouring. Price: €600–€900 per session.

Key differences: Profhilo targets skin quality and hydration with minimal downtime, while Sculptra addresses structural volume loss with longer-lasting but delayed results. Both treatments are complementary and may be combined in a comprehensive facial rejuvenation plan.
