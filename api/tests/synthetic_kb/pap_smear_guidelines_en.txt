Cervical Screening (Pap Smear) Guidelines

Our clinic follows European cervical screening guidelines. Routine screening is recommended every 3 years for women aged 25 to 65. Primary HPV testing is the preferred screening method — samples are tested for high-risk HPV types (16, 18, and others) before cytology is performed. If HPV is detected, reflex cytology (Pap smear) is conducted on the same sample. Women aged 30 and over who test HPV-negative may extend their screening interval to 5 years.

What to expect during the procedure: you will be asked to undress from the waist down and lie on the examination table with your feet in stirrups. A speculum is gently inserted into the vagina to visualise the cervix. A small brush is used to collect cell samples from the cervix — this takes approximately 30 seconds. Some patients experience mild discomfort or a pinching sensation. The entire appointment lasts 10–15 minutes. Results are available within 7–10 working days.

Preparation: avoid sexual intercourse, vaginal douching, and the use of tampons or vaginal medications for 48 hours before your appointment. Schedule the test when you are not menstruating. The test is free of charge within the national screening programme for eligible patients. Private self-pay cost: €80. Abnormal results do not necessarily indicate cancer — they may indicate HPV infection or cellular changes that require monitoring or further investigation via colposcopy.
